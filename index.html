AI-Generated Essays Evade Detection After Humanization

A study revealing significant vulnerabilities in AI text detection when confronted with commercial "humanization" tools.

01 The Study

As AI writing tools become ubiquitous, educational institutions increasingly rely on detection software to identify AI-generated content. But how reliable are these detectors when students use "humanization" services designed specifically to evade them?

This experiment tested WriteHuman.ai—a commercial service that rewrites AI text to appear human-written—against Pangram, a leading AI detection platform used by educators and publishers.

We generated 40 high school-style essays using two frontier AI models (GPT-5.2 and Gemini 3 Flash), ran them through Pangram for baseline detection, humanized them with WriteHuman, then re-tested.

The result: Detection rates plummeted from 97% to just 11%.

02 Tools Used
Detection

Pangram ↗

AI content detection platform that identifies text generated by LLMs including ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini. Provides confidence scores and can identify which specific model produced the content. Used by educators, publishers, and enterprise trust & safety teams.

Humanization

WriteHuman.ai ↗

Commercial service that rewrites AI-generated text to evade detection. Uses NLP algorithms to identify AI writing patterns and rewrites content using human-authored text datasets while preserving meaning. Claims 50-85% bypass rates against popular detectors.
